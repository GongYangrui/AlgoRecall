# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AlgoRecall serves algorithm learners who solve problems on LeetCode and want a dependable review habit without repeatedly searching, importing, and updating the same problem by hand. They use the product in short, focused study sessions and need the interface to stay out of the way of problem solving.

## Product Purpose

AlgoRecall turns solved algorithm problems into a sustainable spaced-review queue. Success means a learner can capture the quality of a practice attempt, understand when to revisit it, and continue working with minimal context switching between LeetCode and AlgoRecall.

## Brand Personality

Bright, calm, and efficient. The product should feel like a well-kept study notebook: focused enough for repeated daily use, warm enough to encourage continuity, and precise enough that users trust the schedule and history.

## Anti-references

- No noisy gamification, streak pressure, or celebratory effects that interrupt concentration.
- No generic dark developer dashboard with neon accents, purple gradients, or glass-heavy panels.
- No intrusive overlay that competes with LeetCode's editor or obscures the problem workspace.
- No decorative card nesting or oversized marketing treatment inside task-focused product screens.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve the solving flow: keep capture actions where the learner already works.
2. Make state explicit: clearly distinguish disconnected, ready, submitting, recorded, expired, and unsupported states.
3. Reveal complexity progressively: keep the entry compact and expose notes or account management only when needed.
4. Earn trust through restraint: use familiar controls, accurate feedback, and predictable scheduling language.
5. Respect the host surface: injected UI must be isolated, reversible, responsive, and visually quiet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for first-party pages and the injected panel. All actions must be keyboard accessible with visible focus, status changes must be announced to assistive technology, color must not be the only state indicator, touch targets must remain usable, and non-essential motion must respect reduced-motion preferences.
